# Douyin Tech Cover Benchmark

Use for technology-knowledge short-video covers, especially AI tools, developer tools, product reviews, and tutorials.

## Core Rules

- Deep/high-contrast background is the default. "Bright" means clear subject and strong contrast, not necessarily light background.
- Keep the visual hierarchy clear: title > subject > background.
- Use at most three dominant colors. White/gold titles on dark backgrounds are common.
- Primary cover title should usually be 4-10 Chinese characters. Keep each visual line short, ideally no more than 5 Chinese characters.
- Cover copy must add information beyond the video title; avoid plain summaries.
- Use bold readable fonts and outlines, especially when a person appears.
- Avoid more than five core visual elements.

## No-Person Covers

- First visual focus: title or one symbolic subject.
- Title often sits in the middle area; product/UI/symbol supports it.
- Good for software tools, product concepts, historical/knowledge topics, and cases where no clean person asset exists.
- Prefer product screenshots, UI silhouettes, code panes, 3D symbols, or real objects over generic stock imagery.

## Person Covers

- Only use real people from the video frames or user-uploaded portrait assets.
- Do not let image generation invent a new presenter or face.
- Person should be a half-body crop, usually centered, with clear expression.
- Title should not cover the face.
- Background should be simpler than no-person covers.

## Title Hooks For AI / Developer Tool Videos

Prefer one of:

- Risk: `用到被封号`, `别急着上手`
- Reversal: `两周后真相`, `真没那么神?`
- Result: `效率真翻倍?`, `真的省时间?`
- Pain point: `别再瞎折腾`, `新手先看`
- Experience proof: `高强度实测`, `踩坑后再说`

Avoid bland titles:

- `Claude使用实测`
- `AI工具介绍`
- `真实感受分享`

## Anti-Patterns

- Fake generated creator portrait
- Extra tiny text, percent labels, layout labels, random UI words
- Title too long or split into unreadable lines
- Too many visual subjects
- Low contrast text
- Platform marks, watermarks, QR codes, fake logos
